A few years ago, I found a program that I knew would change the direction of my career. The kind of opportunity you feel in your gut. I had done the research, spoken to people who had been through it, and I was certain this was it.

"I was watching the deadline get closer, feeling like the opportunity was slipping through my fingers — and there was nothing I could do about it."

Then one evening, completely frustrated, I sat down and did something I had never thought to do before. I scrolled through my phone contacts — all of them — and I started asking myself a simple question about each person: "Would this person give me something small if I asked the right way?"

Not a loan. Not a gift. Just a contribution. A small, specific amount. With a clear reason and a clear deadline.

I spent that night writing messages. Not begging. Not vague. Specific, warm, human messages — sent privately through WhatsApp. Not to a group. Not on a timeline. Person to person.

The money was never the problem. The approach was the problem.

I had been asking the wrong way, on the wrong platforms, with the wrong framing. The moment I changed the method, everything changed.
